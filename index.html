03 — The short version

About

I started on the support side — being the first line of defense when data systems broke — and that gave me a habit that's stuck with me: I care about why something failed, not just that it did. That's carried straight into how I build integrations now.

These days I spend most of my time getting enterprise platforms (SAP, Wrike, SQL Server, DAM systems, EDI feeds) to hand data back and forth reliably, usually via PowerShell, C#, and a fair amount of REST API cleanup. I like the puzzle of it — figuring out where a workflow breaks and building something sturdier in its place.
